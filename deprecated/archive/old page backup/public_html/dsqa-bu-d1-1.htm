Boston University - D1, Class of 2005 

1.  What suggestions do you have to pre-dental students getting ready for dental school?

Definitely take as many upper division classes as possible.  Anatomy, biochemistry, histology, physiology.  That will make your first year much better. 

2.  Do you feel you have made the right decision on the school you are attending? 

Yeah.  All the upper classmen say I'll get good training and everything, but I kind of wish I were on the west coast. 

3.  What are some strengths and weaknesses of the school? 

Hm... 115 people in a class is kind of a lot.  Brand new pre clinical. Sends you out on APEX rotation.  You get good exposure to the practice of dentistry everyday for like 4 months. 

4.  How is the student/teacher ratio?  What is your impression of the faculty? 

Well, lots of the instructors are males, and sometimes I think they pay more attention to the females =)..  I'm not sure. All the science instructors care about you.  As well as the clinical instructors, I think they all do a pretty good job. 

5.  Do you get enough clinical experience? 

I'm just a 1st year, but this semester, we are starting to do operative work.  Drilling, stuff like that.  ON Apex, you will have lots of patient interaction. 

6.  How competitive is the class?  Are classes with medical students? 

Not competitive at all.  Of course some people want to do better, but overall everyone doesn't really care.  Classes are only with dental students, that is definitely a plus.  If you have classes with med students, class will definitely be MUCH harder, b/c as dental students, we don't have to learn bout the hip, or knee or stuff like that as much as med students do.  Yeah, you have gross anatomy and stuff, but I think it focuses more on head and neck of course. 

7.  How's city life and the weather? 

Boston, what can you expect, it is great.  Ultimate college town.
