University of Washington - D1, Class of 2005 

1.  What suggestions do you have to pre-dental students getting ready for dental school?     

Don't worry! like the old saying goes just do your best and don't worry about the results. Get your test scores in, your application and recommendations in early!   aim for a 3.5 GPA and a 20 on your DAT and you're good to go! with that u have an awesome chance of getting  into any dental school.  Dental school is not as hard to get into as people make it out to be..... if U want to be a dentist...U will be one! Make sure you take all the pre-dental classes, if u don't care about a degree just take these classes. You don't need to take anything else, they don't care if you have a degree or not.     if u haven't taken the DAT yet take just take general chemistry, biology, and organic chemistry and as soon as u take these classes take the test, u should take Kaplan... it helps a LOT.  these r the only science subjects on the test, so its all u have to know.  The application process is a headache. At  most... pick 10 never more schools because traveling, interviewing, and keeping track of them all sucks. The interview is nothing.. they just go over what u wrote on your application and ask u to tell them about the things u wrote. they ask also why u want to be a dentist and why u want to go to their school. they never asked me about my weaknesses or any tough questions or questions about the actual profession of dentistry. 

2.  Do you feel you have made the right decision on the school you are attending? 

I'm so glad I'm where I am.. I'm sure I'd be happy at other dental schools too but I'm so glad to be at UW.... the bottom line is no matter where u go.. You'll still be a dentist in the end.. everyone will get the same degree. 

3.  What are some strengths and weaknesses of the school? 

I'm learning so much, but I'm putting in so much. They're teaching us stuff that dentists don't really have to know but medical doctors do... why do I have to learn about the ovaries? if u come here.... you'll be educated as a medical doctor. Of course you'll learn everything about clinical dentistry too. Weaknesses:  Your driven HARD. The good news is, it's hard to flunk out of dental school.

4.  How is the student teacher ratio?  What is your impression of the faculty? 

In didactic courses the student teacher ratio is the same as for undergraduate classes, but for the clinical classes u get a lot of time with your professors... they always just come by and are walking around you. The some of the faculty are nice, but some are ok; you don't do most of your learning from them, its the other people in your class that you ask for help. 

5.  Do you get enough clinical experience? 

Yes! There's plenty 

6.  How competitive is the class?  Are classes with medical students? 

The class is so relaxed; nobody really seems to be competing with one another.. everyone is in it together. The classes with the med students Are cool too. They seem to be more stressed out that dental students, but there is no competition with them.

7.  How's city life and the weather? 

Good old Seattle weather.... oh yeah here at UW .. I don't know how we pulled it off but we have keggers every other week... we love to have fun

8.  I've got some good news for you..

You might have heard that the need for dentists is GROWING.... Dental schools want U!!!! really. There are more retiring dentists than there are people joining the profession. They want U!
