University of Washington - D3, Class of 2003

 1.  What suggestions do you have to predental students getting ready for dental school? 

Getting ready for dental school?  Taking some courses that are completely unrelated to science that you enjoy.  There's a difference between being focused on dental school and having tunnel-vision.

2.  Do you feel you have made the right decision on the school you are  attending? 

The environment that you will be in will differ from class to class.  Some classes are extremely competitive, while others are easy-going.  However, the education here at the UW is top-notch.  The best if you're asking my opinion, within the top 3 if you want the facts.

3.  What are some strengths and weaknesses of the school? 

Superb clinical instructors, graduating dentist are extremely well rounded and well prepared for the boards.  Weaknesses?  Uh... You just have to learn to like getting your butt kicked in academics for the first two years.  Then everything becomes rewarding and satisfying.

4.  How is the student/teacher ratio?  What is your impression of the faculty? 

The faculty are great!

5.  Do you get enough clinical experience? 

You'll be ready when you get out, don't worry about it.  Just focus on getting in this school.  It's not a joy ride.

6.  How competitive is the class?  Are classes with medical students? 

Read above. 

7.  How's city life and the weather?

Get use to it.

8.  If you wish to add additional comments. . . 

 Interviews will screen out those who truly want to become dentist from those who don't.  I've always felt that those who stand the best chance of getting in are those that are diversified and have a well-rounded life coming into dental school.  That's why the UW just doesn't pick the top 55 people with the highest GPA and DAT scores. This is a great time to be getting into dentistry, the demand for dentists is growing.  But make sure you are getting into it knowing that 9 out of 10 will be a general dentist.  Not everyone can do ortho or surgery.  Think... You've taken the best of the best and asked them to rank themselves from 1 to 55 at the end of four years.  You can have a goal in mind, but you should be able to live with other options if things don't work out.
