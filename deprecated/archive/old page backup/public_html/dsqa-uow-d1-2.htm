University of Washington - D1, Class of 2005 

1.  What suggestions do you have to pre-dental students getting ready for dental school?     

I think that it is important to have an excellent background in human anatomy (if Possible).  I know that next quarter our president is working with the undergrad anatomy professor to make the course more preparatory for professional school.  Also, I think a trip or something very relaxing is required to get one last hurrah in before working harder than you ever will. 

2.  Do you feel you have made the right decision on the school you are attending?

Yes. 

3.  What are some strengths and weaknesses of the school? 

At first it seems that the teachers are hazing you by being extra hard, but I think they are just pushing you to do your best. 

4.  How is the student/teacher ratio?  What is your impression of the faculty? 

Most are very nice, although some are not as constructive with their criticism as others.  I don't know what the ratio is, but I have never had any problems getting help. 

5.  Do you get enough clinical experience?    

None yet! 

6.  How competitive is the class?  Are classes with medical students?   

Some people in our class are very competitive, and let you know it (they don't do any better than those of us who take it easy, though). Most of us get along great and help each other out. 

7.  How's city life and the weather?     

Great- you know as well as I do.
