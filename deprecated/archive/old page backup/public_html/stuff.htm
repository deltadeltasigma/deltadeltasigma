Dental Knowledge     

A Dental Implant, in a nutshell

1. Osteointegration occurs as the bone cells (osteoclasts) grow around the titanium implant, securing it into the alveolar ridge. 

2. Once the bone has healed, an abutment is placed to stimulate gingival growth for a more natural appearance.

3. The final restoration is then placed once the gingiva has healed.

 

 

 

The Potential Problem With Wisdom Teeth 

1. Normal, non-problematic, wisdom teeth (3rd molars) erupt just like the other teeth in your mouth. 

2. Sometimes, though, the wisdom teeth are crowed and erupt at various different angles.

3. As the wisdom teeth mature, they crowd the remaining teeth and cause a disruption in tooth alignment or malocclusion. 

 

 

 

The Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ). 

Why Your Jaw Sometimes Pops. 

1. The mandible, (jaw), normally pivots against the skull at the temporomandibular joint. 

2. Sometimes, the joint may separate during movement and actually slide against the skull, instead of pivoting. 

3. When sliding occurs, the jaw may pop and/or cause discomfort.
